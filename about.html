About Cristiana

My research is all about online user behavior. I believe there's an unprecedented value in human digital traces, which can be studied to safeguard society and innovation.

I connect rigorous research with policy-ready insights. My background spans economics, behavioral science, and platform design, equipping me to interrogate how digital systems affect markets and wellbeing.

Academic profile

I am a PhD Candidate in Information Science at Cornell University and an IvyPlus Scholar at Stanford's Department of Economics. I received a BSc and a MSc in Economics and Social Sciences from Bocconi University, where I specialized in Behavioral Economics and Industrial Organization.

I am currently running the Internet Behavior Experiment (IBE) together with Alessandro Acquisti and Cristobal Cheyre and a strong team of researchers.
