About

I am a PhD Candidate in Information Science at Cornell University and an IvyPlus Scholar in the Department of Economics at Stanford University. My research lies at the intersection of economics, behavioral science, and digital platforms.

Academic profile

I study online user behavior and digital market design, with a focus on advertising, algorithmic recommendation systems, and data-driven intermediaries. My work combines field experiments, large-scale behavioral data, and theoretical modeling to examine how platform design choices affect consumer welfare, market outcomes, and privacy.

I received a BSc and an MSc in Economics and Social Sciences from Bocconi University, where I specialized in Behavioral Economics and Industrial Organization.

I am currently co-leading the Internet Behavior Experiment (IBE), a large-scale randomized field experiment designed to study online advertising, tracking, and targeting across browsers, email, and mobile devices, in collaboration with Alessandro Acquisti, Cristobal Cheyre, and an interdisciplinary research team.

Research interests

  • Digital advertising and consumer search
  • Platform design and algorithmic recommendation systems
  • Consumer privacy and data protection
  • Behavioral and empirical industrial organization
  • AI intermediaries and market structure
